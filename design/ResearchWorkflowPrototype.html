Coverage and gaps

Turns analyzer output into an actionable research plan instead of a passive report.

Q1 · Degradation kinetics

partial

Strong data for solar-simulated UV and emulsion systems; weak comparability across dose units.

Normalize exposure units across MED, J/cm², and lamp-specific protocols.
Find finished-product studies rather than neat-solvent systems.

Q2 · Photostabilizers

gaps critical

Evidence conflicts on vitamin E: some studies show stabilization, others report accelerated degradation.

Separate Tocopherol from Tocopheryl Acetate in extracted facts.
Run targeted search for DHHB + Octinoxate co-formulation.

Q3 · Penetration / exposure

complete

Regulatory and ex vivo data are sufficient for a cautious formulation-level answer.

Q4 · Formulation trade-offs

insufficient

Few sources jointly report stability, SPF/UVA-PF, aesthetics, and penetration in the same system.

Need more supplier dossiers or patent examples with full emulsion context.

Verified report draft

Executive summary

Ethylhexyl Methoxycinnamate shows formulation-dependent photodegradation under UV exposure, with emulsion vehicle, co-filter selection, and antioxidant package changing the degradation rate reported across studies F12 F19.

Regulatory evidence supports bounded use under concentration limits, but the formulation question remains partially covered because stability, SPF/UVA-PF, and penetration are rarely measured in the same finished product system F44 F91.

Recommended next step

Run a targeted refinement pass for DHHB + Octinoxate co-formulations and split Tocopherol from Tocopheryl Acetate before treating vitamin E as a single photostabilizer class.
