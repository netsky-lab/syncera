The problem

LLMs are fast. LLMs are also confidently wrong about half of what they cite.

Most "AI research" tools are a single LLM pretending to read papers. Hallucinated citations, missing sources, zero audit trail. You end up re-doing the work in 3× the time.

BROKEN

Fabricated citations

ChatGPT-style tools invent DOIs that look plausible but point to nothing. You only notice when you try to click.

BROKEN

Black-box synthesis

"Trust us, we read 40 papers" — with no visible chain from claim → passage → source. Impossible to audit, impossible to debug.

BROKEN

One-shot outputs

A single LLM call with no verification loop. Every contradiction stays hidden, every nuance gets flattened into the averagiest take.

How it works

Six stages. Each one inspectable, reproducible, and cheap to replay.

Every run is a DAG of small, deterministic tasks. Fail a step, rerun just that step. Change a model, diff the outputs. No magic.

Verification, up close

Every claim ships with a verdict. Not a vibe.

Our NLI verifier runs each atomic claim against all retrieved passages and returns one of four outcomes. The full evidence list is attached — you can click any claim in the brief and jump straight to the supporting passage.

  • ●SUPPORTED≥ 2 sources, no contradicting evidence
  • ●PARTIALSupported with qualifications the brief will surface
  • ●CONTRADICTEDAt least one source disagrees — flagged, not dropped
  • ○UNSUPPORTEDNo evidence found — removed from the brief

Does niacinamide reduce transepidermal water loss?
98 verified claims · 14 contradicted · confidence 0.87
Summary

Topical niacinamide at concentrations between 2% and 5% measurably reduces transepidermal water loss (TEWL) over a treatment window of 4–8 weeks[42][51]. The effect is mediated primarily through upregulation of ceramide and free fatty acid synthesis in the stratum corneum[47], though contradicting evidence exists for penetration kinetics[44].

Key qualifiers

Effect size varies across Fitzpatrick skin types I–VI — only one trial reports stratified data[43]. Formulation matters: emulsion-based vehicles outperform aqueous by ~1.4× on the same concentration[52].

FAQ

Questions smart teams ask first.

How is this different from Perplexity / Elicit / You.com?
Those are query-in, answer-out. We're query-in, traceable DAG-out. Every stage's input, output, and prompt is stored and replay-able. You can fork any run, swap a model at stage 4, and diff the result. Think CI/CD for research.
Can I bring my own sources?
Yes — any retriever with a search endpoint (Elasticsearch, Weaviate, internal wiki, S3 bucket of PDFs). Bring your own index and point us at it.
What about confidential / internal data?
Self-hostable. The whole pipeline runs on-prem with your own model endpoints. We ship a Helm chart and a Terraform module.
Which LLMs do you use?
Claude Sonnet for planning + synthesis, a fine-tuned NLI model for verification, an OSS extractor for claim mining. You can swap any of them via config.
Pricing?
$29 / seat / month for the hosted version, flat. Self-hosted is priced by org, contact us. No per-query billing, no token-metering surprises.
